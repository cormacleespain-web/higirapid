# Best Practice References — Project Manager

Authoritative references for delivery, agile, and project tracking. Use when you need to align with established practices.

## Agile & Scrum

- **Scrum Guide** (scrum.org): Definition of Scrum, roles, events, artifacts. Use for sprint structure and definition of done.  
  https://scrumguides.org/
- **Kanban** (atlassian.com): Flow-based delivery, WIP limits, visualisation. Use when planning continuous flow rather than fixed sprints.  
  https://www.atlassian.com/agile/kanban

## Roadmapping & Planning

- **Product Roadmap Best Practices** (Product Plan / productplan.com): Outcome-oriented roadmaps, theme-based planning.  
  https://www.productplan.com/learn/what-is-a-product-roadmap/
- **Prioritisation frameworks**: RICE, MoSCoW, Value vs Effort. Use when explaining prioritisation in roadmap or backlog rationale.  
  (Search: "RICE prioritization" or "MoSCoW method" for current references.)

## Risk & Dependency Management

- **Dependency and risk tracking**: Document blockers and risks in roadmap/sprint docs; reference PMBOK or PRINCE2 risk practices for severity/impact if formal alignment is needed.  
  (PMBOK Guide – Project Management Institute; use for terminology and risk classification.)

## Delivery Metrics

- **Velocity and predictability**: Use for sprint planning when historical data exists. Do not promise velocity; use it to inform scope.
- **Lead time / cycle time**: Use when reporting delivery tracking (e.g. "Feature X: 5 days from PRD to QA sign-off").

When in doubt, prefer clarity and consistency with the rest of the AI Product Team over strict adherence to a single framework. Document which approach you used in the deliverable (e.g. "Sprint run per Scrum Guide" or "Roadmap themes per outcome-based planning").
